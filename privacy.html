2. Gmail Cleaner — Data Practices

Gmail Cleaner is a Windows desktop application that connects to your Gmail account via Google's official Gmail API to help you bulk delete emails. Here is exactly what the application does and does not do with your data:

What Gmail Cleaner accesses:

  • Your Gmail messages — to search, count, move to Trash, and permanently delete emails matching your chosen filters
  • Your Gmail profile — to display your email address and message count in the app

Why we need full Gmail access scope:

Gmail Cleaner requests the https://mail.google.com/ scope because permanently deleting emails from Gmail requires full mailbox access. Read-only or partial scopes do not permit deletion operations. This scope is used solely to perform the deletion actions you explicitly initiate.

What we never do:

  • We never transmit your emails or email content to any server
  • We never read, store, or log the content of your emails
  • We never share your data with any third party
  • We never use your data for advertising or analytics
  • We never act on your Gmail without your explicit confirmation

Where data is stored:

  • OAuth authentication tokens are stored locally on your device at AppData\Roaming\TapEase\GmailCleaner\tokens\
  • Your account list is stored locally at AppData\Roaming\TapEase\GmailCleaner\accounts.json
  • No data is ever sent to TapEase or any external server

You can revoke Gmail Cleaner's access at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions and removing TapEase Gmail Cleaner.
